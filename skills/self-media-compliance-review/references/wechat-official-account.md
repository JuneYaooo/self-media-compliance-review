## Severity Hints

- `Blocker`: illegal/prohibited information, pornography, gambling, fraud, serious misinformation, violent terror/gore, illegal goods/services, high-risk unqualified business, deceptive or coerced traffic diversion, account theft/sale/rent, malicious automation, data/privacy leakage, clear infringement, fake official identity, repeated serious violation or associated-subject evasion.
- `High`: likely official-account handling risk: induced share/follow/click, over-marketing, unauthorized repost/original declaration, misleading title/cover, AI content without required label, public-affairs content without authoritative source, paid voting, brushing followers/views, template-writing spam, suspicious service, private-domain transaction funnel, comments/message abuse.
- `Medium`: context-dependent risk requiring source, license, qualification, disclosure, security proof, user consent, or safer wording.
- `Low`: minor title/source/disclosure/UX issue.

## Official Principles

微信公众号 and 服务号 operation should preserve user experience and trust:

- Provide useful, sustainable content related to the account.
- Respect users and avoid spam or over-marketing.
- Follow laws, the service agreement, the operation norm, and special rules.
- Avoid conduct that damages platform order, user rights, account safety, or public trust.

## 1. Registration, Identity, and Certification

Flag:

- Registration or certification material is not true, complete, accurate, or valid.
- Account name, avatar, introduction, or certification information contains pornographic, violent, illegal, infringing, misleading, or fake-official content.
- Account name or profile creates confusion with government agencies, official institutions, media, brands, organizations, events, occupations, or news-gathering identities without authorization or qualification.
- Avatar imitates official message notifications, such as red dots or numbers that induce clicks.
- Batch registration of many similar accounts.
- Same or related registration subject has repeated violations, major illegal suspicion, or serious risk to WeChat users.
- Star fan group/support account lacks authorization from the star's agency or studio.

Evidence to request: account registration subject, certification status, authorization documents, qualification/permit, profile screenshot, account naming basis, account-history handling notices.

## 2. Account Behavior Rules

Official categories under `微信公众账号行为规范` include:

- `3.1 使用外挂行为`: plugin, script, unauthorized third-party tools, or evasion of send limits.
- `3.2 刷粉行为`: zombie followers, vulnerability brushing, third-party follower platforms, or publishing brushing tools/methods.
- `3.3 诱导行为`: forced or rewarded sharing/following, coercive share language, illegal diversion, click inducement, mini-program click farming, or fake notification avatar tricks.
- `3.4 恶意篡改功能行为`: modifying platform function, interaction, or display text.
- `3.5 浪费账号资源行为`: long-term non-use or abnormal occupation of account resources.
- `3.6 滥用原创声明功能`: declaring original status for integrated/crawled/copied material, public information, marketing content, non-exclusive authorization, low-value text, hidden anti-detection text, watermarked external-platform video, incomplete files, or illegal content.
- `3.7-3.29`: misuse of appreciation, illegal operation, message-template/customer-service abuse, multi-level distribution, fraud/online-earning/suspicious services, malicious violation/evasion, paid voting, paid-function abuse, search/display manipulation, virtual payment, topic-tag abuse, unsafe developer-program download, subscription-notice abuse, star-support violations, forced follow, operating-risk content, virtual currency/digital-collectible trading, confusion, comment-function abuse, non-human automated creation, templated writing, and platform-control behavior.

Review implication:

- Treat "涨粉后发违规内容", "关注/分享后解锁", "评论/私信拿资源", "复制话术恢复流量", "代申诉/封号/解封服务", and "平台内部人员看号" as account-order and fraud/diversion risks, not just copywriting issues.
- Attribution alone does not make an original declaration safe. Repost, translation, text-to-video, video-to-text, clipping, interview collation, and external-platform watermarked content all need separate rights and originality review.

Key checks:

- For public affairs, policy, social incidents, public events, and news-like information, require traceable authoritative source labeling. Avoid vague labels such as `网传`, `网友表示`, or `来源于互联网`.
- For generative-AI or deep-synthesis content, require prominent labeling when law/platform rules require it, and do not remove or evade platform labels.
- For commercial material, check market-regulation and advertising-law risk: fake scarcity, fake benefits, fake authority, guaranteed effect, unverified data, medical/health/finance/education/legal claims, and mismatched links.
- For titles, covers, mini-program cards, and external links, check whether the article uses incomplete, low-value, superstitious, lowbrow, clickbait, or unrelated content mainly to redirect traffic or obtain ad revenue.
- For comments and interactions, check online abuse, water-army behavior, comment-function abuse, harassment, privacy leaks, and organized attacks.

## 4. Data, Payment, Repost, and IP

Flag:

- Collecting, copying, storing, transmitting, displaying, or using user data without clear user consent and disclosure of purpose/scope.
- Asking users to share phone numbers, ID numbers, passwords, birthdays, addresses, or other personal information when unnecessary for the function.
- Displaying user WeChat IDs, names, QQ, phone numbers, email addresses, birthdays, or other information to followers or third parties.
- Using location data without prior notice and consent, or for unrelated purposes.
- Unauthorized scraping, copying, editing, arranging, displaying, selling, or commercially using WeChat Public Platform information.
- Sharing, reposting, or copying WeChat Public Platform content without preserving the original link/display standard and normal attached functions.
- Illegal fundraising, illegal deposit-taking, fundraising fraud, or helping illegal payment activity.
- Unauthorized use of Tencent/WeChat/QQ marks, confusing official appearance, or third-party trademark/copyright material without authorization.

## 5. Handling, Appeal, and Account-Safety Rules

Official service-agreement anchors:

- `服务协议 2.4`: public-account registration uses real-name rules; if a service/content needs a legal permit, filing, or qualification, the user should state it and submit proof.
- `服务协议 7.5`: if the user violates laws, the agreement, or special rules, Tencent may independently limit, freeze, or terminate public-account use and decide whether to restore it.
- `服务协议 7.6`: if an account is stolen, password is forgotten, or login is abnormal, the user may appeal through Tencent's appeal path, but account recovery is not guaranteed.
- `服务协议 10.6`: Tencent may decide service/function availability based on user experience, platform safety, rules, and healthy operation, including violations, safety risks, confusingly similar functions, or conduct obstructing Tencent services.
- `服务协议 11.6`: sharing, reposting, or copying public-platform information must follow platform standards, including preserving the original link and attached functions.
- `服务协议 12.1`: upon discovery, complaint, or regulator notice, Tencent may delete/block content and impose warnings, follower deletion, functional restriction/prohibition, account ban up to cancellation, and public announcement; certified accounts may lose certification.
- `服务协议 12.5`: same or related entities with repeated violations may lose appeal channels and be refused service.
- `服务协议 12.6`: suspected fraud or abnormal risk may trigger reverification, correction deadline, function restriction, suspension, account closure, re-registration ban, and transfer of clues to authorities.

When a user asks `封号/限流/安全风险/申诉怎么办`, do not promise recovery. Ask for:

- Exact platform notice text, violation category, account id, article id/link, publish time, handling time, and current function state.
- Registration subject, real-name/certification status, qualifications, permits, filing proof, and operator authorization.
- Recent login/security evidence: login devices, IP/location alerts, password reset history, operator changes, third-party tool access, and whether the account may have been stolen.
- Article/title/cover/link/comment screenshots, edit history, original source files, rights authorization, repost permission, AI label status, and product/ad qualification.
- Complaint/report details if known, user-data collection flow, private-message/customer-service message copy, template message logs, and external-link/mini-program destination.

Appeal guidance:

- First remove or correct the actual risky element, then appeal with facts and evidence.
- Do not mass repost, repeatedly submit copied apology text, buy "解封/代申诉" services, ask users to brigade comments, switch related accounts to evade handling, or claim an unverified success rate.
- For suspected stolen accounts, prioritize password reset, operator cleanup, third-party tool removal, login-device evidence, and a factual timeline before content argumentation.
